[PHTITLE]CONDITIONAL PERFECT TENSE


Use the conditional perfect when discussing situations in the past which are contrary to the fact, i.e. when talking about something that would have happened: 

	Si hubiera ido a la fiesta, habría visto a Isabel. 
	(If I had gone to the party, I would have seen Isabel.)
 
	Yo le habría hablado a mi hermano. 
	 (I would have talked to my brother.)


[PHSECTION]To form the conditional perfect, use: 
          habría / habrías / habría / habríamos / habríais / habrían   + past participle

     Yo habría caminado      [I would have walked.]
     Tú habrías caminado      [You would have walked.]
     Ella / Él / Usted habría caminado      [She would have walked.]
     Nosotros habríamos caminado      [We would have walked.]
     Vosotros habríais caminado      [You all would have walked.]
     Ellos / Ustedes habrían caminado      [They would have walked.]
