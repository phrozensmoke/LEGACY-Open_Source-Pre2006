[PHTITLE]ESTAR VS. SER

[PHSECTION]Use of 'Estar':
  
[PH-HI]To tell location.[/PH-HI]
	El libro está en la mesa.  (The book is on the table.)

[PH-HI]To describe health.[/PH-HI]
	Estoy muy bien.  (I am very well.)

[PH-HI]With adjectives to describe conditions.[/PH-HI]
	 Estoy muy ocupado.  (I am very worried.)

[PH-HI]In a number of fixed expressions.[/PH-HI]
	Estoy de acuerdo.   (I agree.)
	Está bien.  (It's good./It's okay.)

[PH-HI]To form present participle.[/PH-HI]
	Estoy estudiando ahora mismo.  (I am studying right now.)

  
[PHSECTION]Use of 'Ser': 
  
[PH-HI]To identify people and things.[/PH-HI]
	Yo soy estudiante. (I am a student.)
	Esto es un libro.  (This is a book.)
	Alicia y yo somos amigos.  (Alicia and I are friends.)

[PH-HI]To express nationality; with 'de' to express origin.[/PH-HI]
	Son cubanos.  (They are Cubans.)
	Son de la Habana. (They are from Havana.)
	¿De donde es Ud?  (Where are you from?)

[PH-HI]With 'de' to express of what material is something made.[/PH-HI]
	Este bolígrafo es de plástico  (The pen is made of plastic.)

[PH-HI]With 'para' to tell for whom or what is something intended.[/PH-HI]
	Es para la clase de inglés.  (It's for the English class.)
	Es regalo para Sara.  (It's a gift for Sara.)

[PH-HI]To tell time.[/PH-HI]
	Son las once.  (It's 11 o'clock.)
	Es la una y media.  (It's 1:30.)

[PH-HI]With de to express possesion.[/PH-HI]
	Es de Carlota.  (It's Carlota's.)

[PH-HI]With adjectives to describe basic, inherent qualities or characteristics of people and things.[/PH-HI]
	Soy sentimental.  (I am sentimental.)
	El coche es muy viejo.  (The car is very old.)

[PH-HI]To express generalizations (only with es).[/PH-HI]
	Es importante estudiar, pero no es necesario estudiar todos los días.  (It's important to study, but it is not necessary to study everyday.)


[PH-HI]The main difference is that estar refers to temporary attributes while ser to permanent or more lasting ones.[/PH-HI]
