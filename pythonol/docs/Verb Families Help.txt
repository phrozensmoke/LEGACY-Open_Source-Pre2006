[PHTITLE]VERB FAMILIES

Click the [PH-HI]'Verb Families'[/PH-HI] tab.

The 'Verb Families' tab shows a list of approximately 90 known Spanish verb families.  [PH-HI]A 'verb family' is a group of verbs which are all conjugated in a similar manner.[/PH-HI]  For example, the verb 'poner' (to put) is part of a 'family' that contains such verbs as 'oponer', 'yuxtaponer' and 'suponer'. All of these verbs are conjugated in a similary manner:

	[PH-HI]PONER:[/PH-HI]
		Yo pongo.  (I put.)
	[PH-HI]SUPONER:[/PH-HI]
		Yo supongo.  (I suppose.)
	[PH-HI]OPONER:[/PH-HI]
		Yo opongo.  (I oppose.)
	[PH-HI]YUXTAPONER:[/PH-HI]
		Yo yuxtapongo.  (I juxtapose.)

The browseable list of 'verb families' is intended for your informational and studying purposes. You may save the list of 'verb families' to a plain text file by clicking the [PH-HI]'Save To File'[/PH-HI] button in the 'Verb Families' section.  Referring to the 'verb family' list periodically will greatly improve your Spanish vocabulary and speed up your learning of Spanish verb conjugation and grammar.

If you are running a Linux/Unix version of Pythoñol, and have an LRPng/LPR-based spooler system on your computer, you may [PH-HI]print[/PH-HI] the list of 'verb families' by clicking [PH-HI]'File' -> 'Print...'[/PH-HI] on the Pythoñol main menu.  (Sorry, Windows users!)   See [PH-HI]'Help' --> 'Help...'[/PH-HI] on the menu for more information on printing in Pythoñol.

If you have the [PH-HI]Festival text-to-speech[/PH-HI] extension installed and usable, you may hear the words pronounced by clicking the light blue [PH-HI]'Play'[/PH-HI] button, generally located to the left of your screen.  Click [PH-HI]'Help' -> 'Festival Text-to-Speech Help...'[/PH-HI] on the menu for more information.
